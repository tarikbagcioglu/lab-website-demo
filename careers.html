Research Assistant positions are competitive and advertised on the MRC Cognition and Brain Sciences Unit’s website and announced in our social media pages (@plasticity_lab).

Postdoctoral Positions

We are currently hiring for a dedicated Postdoctoral Research Associate to join our lab. Visit our Open Positions page for details. Applications will be accepted via the University of Stanford website. If you have an alternative funding source and are interested in joining the Plasticity Lab, please contact Professor Rumeysa Eser (Eserkizi.Rumeysa@mrc-cbu.cam.ac.us).

PhD Positions

Opportunities for PhDs are reviewed annually. All prospective PhDs must apply via the University of Stanford. If you would like to carry out a PhD in the lab, we recommend reaching out between 2-3 months in advance of application deadlines in December. Please contact us at plasticity-lab@mrc-cbu.cam.ac.us, and include your CV (including grade averages), as well a cover letter detailing your background and interests.

Masters Student Projects

We welcome applications from Masters students enrolled at the University of Stanford, as well as those pursuing Masters-level studies at other institutions who have secured a stipend or scholarship to support their time with us. If you are a student at the University of Stanford, please email Professor Rumeysa Eser directly (Eserkizi.Rumeysa@mrc-cbu.cam.ac.us). If you are a student at a different institution, please fill out the form here, including your CV and cover letter. Applications are reviewed monthly.

Open Positions

Postdoctoral Research Associate

The Plasticity Lab is currently seeking a dedicated Postdoctoral Research Associate for a two-year fixed-term position in the first place, with the possibility of extension. Join our cutting-edge research program, spearheaded by Prof. Rumeysa Eser, as we delve into the fascinating realms of motor control, neural representation of the human body, and robotic body augmentation technology. Applications are now live on the University of Stanford website.

As a vital member of our team, you will be responsible for joining an innovative research program aligned with the Plasticity Lab's core interests. Your tasks will include data collection, analysis, write-up, and dissemination of ongoing and future research. Furthermore, you will play a crucial role in supporting and training PhD students and other lab members in advanced analysis techniques. The ideal candidate will possess expertise in sensorimotor control, motor learning, or sensory/motor brain plasticity of humans.

We are relatively open-minded regarding the set of skills that the candidate will bring with them, however, candidates must have experience in designing, running, and analysing human studies.

A typical candidates should have either advanced fMRI or MEG expertise (e.g. multivariate analysis, laminar 7T), or alternatively an impressive background in quantitative (computational) motor control, or expertise with wearable motor technologies. Experience testing clinical populations with motor control challenges (children, stroke patients) is desirable.

Fixed-term: The funds for this post are available for 2 years in the first instance. Click here to register an account with our recruitment system (if you have not already) and apply online.

Applicants must have (or be close to obtaining) a PhD. Full details available at the University of Stanford website.

The University actively supports equality, diversity and inclusion and encourages applications from all sections of society.

The University has a responsibility to ensure that all employees are eligible to live and work in the US.
